# How to Buy a House in the Philippines: Complete Guide

Buying your first home is one of the biggest financial decisions you'll ever make. This guide walks you through every step of the process in the Philippines.

## Step 1: Assess Your Financial Readiness

Before you start looking at properties, understand your financial situation:

- **Income**: Can you afford monthly payments? A general rule is that housing costs should not exceed 30% of your monthly income.
- **Savings**: You'll need at least 20% for down payment plus closing costs.
- **Credit Score**: Check your credit history with the Credit Information Corporation (CIC).

## Step 2: Get Pre-Approved for a Home Loan

Types of financing available:

| Type | Best For | Interest Rate |
|------|----------|---------------|
| **Pag-IBIG Housing Loan** | Government employees | 6.5-10.5% |
| **Bank Housing Loan** | Salaried employees | 7-12% |
| **In-House Financing** | Self-employed | 12-18% |
| **SSS Housing Loan** | SSS members | 8-10.5% |

### Requirements Typically Needed:
- Valid ID (at least 2)
- Certificate of Employment with Compensation (CEC)
- Latest ITR (Income Tax Return)
- Payslips (last 3 months)
- Birth Certificate (PSA)
- Marriage Certificate (if applicable)

## Step 3: Find the Right Property

Consider these factors:

1. **Location** — Proximity to work, schools, hospitals, and public transportation
2. **Size** — Number of bedrooms, lot area, floor area
3. **Type** — House and lot, condo, townhouse, or lot only
4. **Budget** — Include all costs: down payment, monthly amortization, association dues, property tax
5. **Future Value** — Will the property appreciate over time?

## Step 4: Due Diligence

Before making an offer:

- **Verify the Title** — Check with the Registry of Deeds
- **Check for Encumbrances** — Ensure no liens or mortgages
- **Inspect the Property** — Physical inspection for structural issues
- **Verify the Developer** — If buying new, check PSHDA registration
- **Flood History** — Check if the area is flood-prone

## Step 5: Make an Offer and Close the Deal

1. Submit a Letter of Intent (LOI)
2. Negotiate the price and terms
3. Sign the Contract to Sell (CTS)
4. Pay the down payment
5. Apply for housing loan
6. Sign the Contract to Buy and Sell (CTBS)
7. Transfer the title
8. Pay all fees and taxes
9. Get your keys!

## Fees to Expect

| Fee | Approximate Cost |
|-----|-----------------|
| Down Payment | 10-20% of total price |
| Transfer Tax | 0.5-0.75% of property value |
| Registration Fee | 0.25% of property value |
| Notarial Fees | ₱1,000-5,000 |
| Title Insurance | 0.5% of property value |
| Agent Commission | 3-5% (paid by seller) |
